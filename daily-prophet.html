Breaking from the Muggle Realm
Young Wizard Claims Silver
at GDGHacks Tournament
Mkareos Kzkoz, a 2nd-year computing apprentice at the University of Guelph, has stunned judges with a “Cutting Edge UI” spell the likes of which the Muggle tech world has never witnessed.
Mkareos Kzkoz, moments after the judges’ announcement — GDGHacks 2025, University of Guelph

In a remarkable display of enchantment and engineering, Mkareos Kzkoz — known in these halls as MK — emerged from the gruelling GDGHacks 2025 competition with the silver distinction of 2nd Place, earning the coveted Best Cutting Edge UI award for his project RankedIn.

“No AI APIs. Fully deterministic. 0–2500 scoring across 8 dimensions.”

RankedIn, described by sources close to the Ministry as “LinkedIn’s Aura Score leaderboard,” calculates a wizard’s professional standing using a proprietary 8-dimensional formula — without so much as a whisper of AI sorcery. Sources confirm the judges were bewitched by the interface alone, praising its speed and clarity under tournament conditions.

The competition, hosted by Google Developer Groups, drew some of the finest young enchanters from across the region. MK’s team built and shipped the project within the span of a single moonrise-to-moonrise cycle, a feat our correspondents describe as “borderline Dark Arts, but legal.”
